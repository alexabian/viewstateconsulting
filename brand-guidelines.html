Brand Identity Guidelines

Introduction

Viewstate Consulting is a Dubai-based IT infrastructure consulting and fractional technology leadership firm. Our brand identity reflects our core values: precision, clarity, expertise, and modern thinking.

These guidelines ensure consistent application of our brand across all touchpoints. Every element has been carefully considered to communicate professionalism, technical expertise, and timeless sophistication.

Design Philosophy

Our visual identity draws inspiration from three key sources:

  • Nordic Minimalism — Function over decoration, clarity over complexity
  • Dieter Rams' Principles — Good design is as little design as possible
  • IBM 1970s Aesthetic — Paul Rand's iconic striped logo approach

Logo

Logo Usage

Clear Space

Maintain minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the letter "V" in the logo. This ensures the logo remains legible and impactful.

Minimum Size

To maintain legibility of the striped effect:

  • Horizontal logo: minimum width of 120px
  • Stacked logo: minimum width of 80px
  • Monogram: minimum width of 24px

Usage Rules

Do

  • Use provided logo files only
  • Maintain aspect ratio
  • Use black on light backgrounds
  • Use white on dark backgrounds
  • Ensure sufficient contrast

Don't

  • Distort or stretch the logo
  • Change the stripe pattern
  • Add shadows or effects
  • Place on busy backgrounds
  • Use colors other than black/white

Design Principles

Every design decision should align with these principles, inspired by Dieter Rams:

1. Good design is innovative

We use modern techniques and technologies while respecting timeless principles.

2. Good design makes a product useful

Every element serves a purpose. If it doesn't communicate or function, remove it.

3. Good design is aesthetic

Beauty through simplicity and proportion, not decoration.

4. Good design makes a product understandable

Clear hierarchy, obvious navigation, intuitive interactions.

5. Good design is unobtrusive

The design should support content, not compete with it.

6. Good design is honest

No visual gimmicks or misleading effects. Authentic representation.

7. Good design is long-lasting

Avoid trends. Create designs that will look appropriate in 10 years.

8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail

Nothing is arbitrary. Every pixel is considered.

9. Good design is environmentally friendly

Optimized code, fast loading, minimal resources.

10. Good design is as little design as possible

Less, but better. Concentrate on the essential aspects.

Voice & Tone

Brand Voice

  • Clear and direct — No fluff or marketing speak
  • Confident without arrogance — We know our stuff, but we're approachable
  • Technical but accessible — Explain complex topics simply
  • Results-focused — Emphasize outcomes over process
  • Professional but human — Avoid corporate jargon

Writing Examples

Do Write

  • "We solve complex IT problems faster using AI-assisted workflows"
  • "15 years managing IT operations across 10+ countries"
  • "We embed with your team and deliver practical solutions"

Don't Write

  • "We leverage cutting-edge synergistic solutions to optimize your digital transformation journey"
  • "Extensive experience in multinational IT leadership"
  • "We partner with stakeholders to ideate innovative strategies"
